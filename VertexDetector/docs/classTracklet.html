Detailed Description

Tracklet class.

Used to initialise a Tracklet, which is a line joining two hits (data members fHits1L and fHits2L. These hits are Points located on two different layers of the detector. The tracklet can be accepted, or not, as a viable candidate for a high momentum Particle's trajectory according to geometrical constraints defined in the method matchHits(). If the tracklet is accepted, then it is used to extrapolate the reconstructed vertex position: the intersection between the tracklet and the beam line (see extractVertex()).

◆ extractVertex()

Point Tracklet::extractVertex ( )

Extrapolates the reconstructed vertex position (z coordinate only) on the beam line.

A line joining the two hits on the two detector's layers is used to extrapolate the z coordinate of the reconstructed vertex. The x and y coordinates of the reconstructed vertex are set to 0. (see the constraint decribed in matchHits() for an explanation). The uncertainty on the reconstructed z coordinate is propagated taking into account the smearing of the hits on the detector's layers.

◆ matchHits()

Bool_t Tracklet::matchHits ( )

Checks whether the Tracklet is to be accepted.

A Tracklet is accepted only if the distance between the azimutal φ coordinates of the two hits (Tracklet's data members) is <= cutPhi. cutPhi is hardcoded in ~/VertexDetector/Geometry/Detector.h and is a variable of the namespace detector. Its value is set to 0.01 rad and let us consider negligible the distance in φ between the two hits of accepted tracklets.
